At the beginning of my time with the UT Aeroelasticity Lab, I was put onto the Full Airframe Sensing Technology (FAST) Project. This project used a conceptual high speed aerospace vehicle called the initial concept 3x (IC3X) as the framework for forming a state estimation of the aircraft from discrete sensors of different types.

**Wind Tunnel Model Design**
At the beginning, I was tasked with redesigning a few different aspects of the vehicle for use in the Mach 5 wind tunnel at UT Austin. This included blunting the sharpness of the control surfaces further, as well as making a simple connection between the vehicle and the sting that was used to hold the model. In doing this, I was able to develop my skills in computer aided design (CAD), as well as facing problems that would not be anticipated in low speed cases like higher amplitude vibrations and forces from shocks.

**Testing and Sensor Familiarization**
I also participated in testing on both a benchtop and wind tunnel scale model of the IC3X. For both models, the lab used a Fiber Bragg Grating (FBG) system for sensing strain. For the former, I was able to use a test configuration that allowed for both thermal inputs and loads, and I assisted in collecting readings for both. However, for the latter, I was tasked with installing and calibrating the FBG sensors, and from this experience I gained a lot of intuition on how and where measurements are best done for strain and deformation. Also, the hands on experience allowed me to develop an appreciation for being meticulous and careful, as the fibers I worked with were rather fragile and finnicky if not handled correctly.

**Strain to Deformation Algorithms and FEA**
During the course of the project, I was also able to learn about modal analysis and finite element analysis. The method of converting strain to deformation used strain and deformation mode shapes of a model to predict deformations based on discrete strain sensor readings. To test and refine this method, collection of theoretical strain and deformation data was required, and I was tasked with running FEA simulations to do so. In the process, I learned about the steps and requirements of such a process, and how to properly set up such a simulation. I also learned about the structural dynamics behind it all, which is knowledge I have been able to apply further in the FEA that I have done since. Presentations that were given about this part of the project can be found in the “Papers and Presentations” tab.
